Ségolène Martin


I am a PhD student in applied mathematics at CentraleSupelec, Université Paris-Saclay, in the CVN / OPIS Inria team. I started my PhD in September 2020 under the supervision of Jean-Christophe Pesquet (CVN, CentraleSupelec) and Ismail Ben Ayed (ILLS, ETS Montréal).

My thesis focuses on the development of new optimization-based methods for solving large-scale data-driven problems, such as constrained inverse problems in imaging and image classification. I am particularly interested in studying the convergence and robustness of the algorithms, and coupling them with learning techniques. On the application part, I work with medical images (histopathology, PET) and volumes from multiphoton microscops.

Research interests

My research interest lie at the intersection of optimization and machine learning, with applications to image processing and computer vision. More specifically:

  • Continuous large-scale optimization (majoration-minimization, proximal, subspace, alternating methods, convex and non-convex optimization)
  • Inverse problems in imaging (image restoration and reconstruction, PSF estimation)
  • Optimization-based modeling and algorithm design for clustering and classification (few-shot learning, transform learning, unbalanced learning, foundation models)
